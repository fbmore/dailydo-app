## Description (4000 chars max)

Everything in your head, in one place.

DailyDo is the fastest way to get a thought out of your head and into the right place. Type it, paste it, dictate it, or share it from any app — DailyDo instantly recognizes what it is and files it as a task, note, link, timer, or document. No folders to choose, no forms to fill, no account to create.

SMART CAPTURE
• Write "pick up prescription tomorrow at 5pm" → a task with a reminder, set.
• Paste a URL → a link, with the page title fetched automatically.
• Jot a long thought → a note, titled for you.
• Everything lands sorted and searchable.

CAPTURE FROM ANYWHERE
• Share into DailyDo from Safari, Photos, Mail, or any app.
• Smart paste from the clipboard.
• Widgets for one-tap capture from your Home Screen.

TASKS THAT HOLD THE LINE
• Due-time reminders with snooze and mark-done right from the notification.
• Organize by today, upcoming, and someday.
• Checklists inside tasks and notes — shareable with a link.

MORE THAN A TODO LIST
• Notes with automatic titling.
• Links with fetched titles — a reading list that builds itself.
• Multi-stage timers for cooking, workouts, and focus sessions.
• Documents: scan with automatic text recognition (OCR), snap photos, attach files.
• Voice notes with automatic transcription.
• Tags with colors to slice it all your way.

PRIVATE BY ARCHITECTURE
• No account. No sign-up. No analytics. No ads.
• Your data lives on your device and syncs through your own private iCloud.
• The developer has no servers and no access to anything you capture.

FOR IPHONE, IPAD, AND MAC
Your items follow you everywhere, synced seamlessly and privately through iCloud.

DailyDo is for people whose day doesn't fit in a todo app: the errand and the article, the idea and the receipt, the timer for the pasta and the reminder for the call. Stop deciding where things go. Just capture — DailyDo does the rest.

## App Privacy (nutrition label answers)
- Data collection: **Data Not Collected** (no analytics, no accounts, no third-party SDKs; user content stays in the user's private CloudKit database, which Apple treats as not "collected" by the developer).
- Verify before submitting: no crash-reporting SDK, and URL title fetching goes device → target site directly.

## Review Notes (App Review "Notes" field suggestion)
> DailyDo requires no account. All data is stored locally and synced via the user's private CloudKit database. Notifications are used for user-created task reminders and timer completions. Camera/microphone/speech permissions are requested contextually for document scanning, photo capture, and voice notes with on-device transcription. To test sync, sign in to iCloud on the simulator/device.
